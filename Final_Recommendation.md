# Final Recommendation

## Executive Summary

Based on the regression analysis, the multiple regression model was selected as the final model because it explained a large proportion of the variation in monthly sales and provided meaningful business insights.

## 1. Factors Most Strongly Associated with Monthly Sales

The analysis identified the following variables as the strongest drivers of monthly sales:

* **Footfall** – Stores with higher customer visits generated higher sales.
* **Marketing Spend** – Increased marketing investment was positively associated with sales growth.
* **Inventory Availability Percentage** – Stores with better inventory availability achieved higher sales.
* **Staff Count** – Adequate staffing levels contributed positively to sales performance.
* **Customer Rating** – Higher customer satisfaction was associated with increased sales.

These variables showed positive coefficients and statistically significant p-values (p < 0.05), indicating strong associations with monthly sales.

---

## 2. Variables Leadership Should Focus On

Leadership should prioritize:

1. Increasing customer footfall through targeted promotions and local marketing campaigns.
2. Optimizing marketing spend in high-performing stores and regions.
3. Maintaining high inventory availability to avoid stock-outs and lost sales.
4. Ensuring appropriate staffing levels, particularly during peak and holiday periods.
5. Improving customer experience to increase customer ratings and repeat purchases.

---

## 3. Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

Some variables showed weak statistical evidence or higher p-values and should be interpreted cautiously.

Examples include:

* Average Discount Percentage
* Certain regional dummy variables
* Certain store-type categories

These variables may not have a strong or consistent direct relationship with sales.

---

## 4. Recommended Business Actions

Based on the regression findings, the following actions are recommended:

### Marketing Strategy

Increase marketing investment in stores and regions demonstrating strong returns on marketing spend.

### Inventory Management

Improve inventory planning and stock replenishment processes to maintain high product availability.

### Staffing Optimization

Allocate additional staff to stores experiencing high customer footfall or seasonal demand.

### Customer Experience

Invest in employee training, service quality, and customer engagement initiatives to improve customer ratings.

### Regional Prioritization

Prioritize investment in regions that consistently outperform others according to the regression results.

---

## 5. Risks and Limitations

Leadership should consider the following limitations:

* The model includes only variables available in the dataset.
* External factors such as local economic conditions, competitor actions, seasonality, and promotional campaigns were not fully captured.
* The model assumes linear relationships among variables.
* Some variables may be correlated with one another, potentially affecting coefficient interpretation.

---

## 6. Why Regression Does Not Prove Causation

Regression analysis identifies statistical associations between variables but does not automatically prove cause-and-effect relationships.

For example, higher marketing spend may be associated with higher sales, but this does not guarantee that marketing alone caused the increase. Other factors, such as store location, seasonality, or customer demographics, may also influence sales.

Therefore, regression results should be combined with business knowledge, experimentation, and additional analysis before making major strategic decisions.

---

## Final Recommendation

The multiple regression model should be adopted as the primary decision-support tool. Leadership should focus on improving customer footfall, marketing effectiveness, inventory availability, staffing levels, and customer satisfaction to maximize monthly sales performance.
